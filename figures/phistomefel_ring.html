★ Theorem (Proven). In any valid completed Sudoku, ms(G) = ms(R) where R = I ∪ C = 12 edge-box inner cells (I) + 4 corner-box center cells (C).

Proof: Inclusion-exclusion on outer rows {0,1,7,8} and outer columns {0,1,7,8} yields ms(G) = 8×[1-9] - ms(S) where S = outer rows ∪ outer cols. Decomposing S by boxes and canceling Box 4's contribution gives ms(G) = ms(C) + ms(I) = ms(R). Verified on 6 independent solutions.
🔬 Non-unique. These 25 patterns are solution-dependent (0/25 cross-validate on other solutions). They exist because the 9 degrees of freedom in the multiset allow many 16-cell configurations with the same digit counts. The universal ring R = I ∪ C is the only pattern guaranteed for all solutions.
⚠️ Generalization refuted. The "away-from-center" rule does not generalize to arbitrary complementary 4-box partitions. 70/70 non-classic partitions produce different multisets.
